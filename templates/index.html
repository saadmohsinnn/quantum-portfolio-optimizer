Quantum concepts explained

What is a qubit?

The basic unit of quantum information. Unlike a classical bit (0 or 1), a qubit can exist in a superposition of both states until measured. Here, each stock is encoded as one qubit: 1 = hold, 0 = don't hold.

Superposition

Qubits can be 0 and 1 at the same time. This lets the quantum circuit explore many portfolio combinations at once, instead of trying them one by one—a form of quantum parallelism.

Entanglement

Qubits can be linked so that measuring one affects others instantly. This captures correlations between stocks: when one moves, others may move together.

VQE (Variational Quantum Eigensolver)

A hybrid quantum-classical algorithm that finds the best portfolio by minimizing a cost function. It runs a parameterized quantum circuit and uses a classical optimizer to tune those parameters.

Why quantum for finance?

Portfolio optimization is combinatorially hard. Quantum algorithms offer potential speedup and better solutions for complex optimizations as hardware improves.
